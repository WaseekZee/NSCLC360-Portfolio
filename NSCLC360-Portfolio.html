Introduction

NSCLC 360 is a multidisciplinary research initiative focused on Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC), which accounts for approximately 85% of all lung cancer cases globally. Our project aims to revolutionize the prognosis and treatment planning process by leveraging multiomics data—including genomics, proteomics, and imaging—combined with advanced machine learning techniques.

Despite medical advancements, the complexity of NSCLC poses significant challenges for personalized treatment. Our research seeks to address this by integrating diverse biological data sources to provide a 360-degree perspective on each patient's condition. By applying explainable AI models, we aim to enhance decision-making in early diagnosis, risk stratification, and therapy response prediction.

Ultimately, NSCLC 360 aspires to contribute to the future of precision oncology by offering data-driven insights that empower clinicians and researchers in the fight against lung cancer.

Our Domain

Lung cancer remains the leading cause of cancer-related deaths worldwide, with Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) accounting for nearly 85% of all cases. Traditional diagnostic methods rely heavily on radiology and pathology, which often fail to capture the complex biological variability among patients. With the rapid evolution of biomedical technology, multiomics data—integrating genomics, proteomics, and radiomics—provides an unprecedented opportunity to understand cancer at a systems level. Our domain focuses on applying machine learning to this integrated data to uncover hidden patterns, predict prognosis, and support clinical decisions.

While multiomics data is increasingly available, existing approaches often analyze these modalities in isolation. There is a lack of unified, explainable machine learning models that can integrate heterogeneous datasets to deliver actionable insights in real-world NSCLC prognosis. Furthermore, many AI models remain "black boxes", offering predictions without transparent reasoning—a critical barrier in clinical adoption.

  • Lack of integrated frameworks for multiomics-based prognosis in NSCLC.
  • Limited interpretability in current ML models used for lung cancer prediction.
  • Challenges in managing class imbalance and data sparsity in clinical datasets.
  • Insufficient early warning systems for complication prediction post-diagnosis.

  • Develop an AI-powered framework for NSCLC prognosis using multiomics data fusion.
  • Incorporate explainable AI techniques (e.g., SHAP, LIME) to interpret predictions.
  • Evaluate model performance on real-world clinical datasets from TCIA and LIDC-IDRI.
  • Visualize and report the key biological features influencing patient outcomes.

What Do We Provide

Early Detection & Feature Extraction

Implements 3D ResNet-18 on PET/CT scans for tumor localization and TNM classification using spatially enhanced DICOM volumes.

Multiomics Prognostic Modeling

Uses TCGA-LUAD genomic data and L1-penalized Cox models to identify prognostic biomarkers and survival correlations.

Complication Prediction

Predicts post-diagnosis complications using PLCO clinical data and ensemble ML models such as Random Forest and TabNet.

Recurrence & Survival Modeling

Combines Random Forest and CoxPH models to predict recurrence type and progression-free intervals from genomic and phenotype data.
